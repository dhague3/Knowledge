The Hidden CMS Trap

Each team uses its own CMS, each claiming ownership of taxonomy and product terms. You end up with conflicting versions of "truth" for brand, tone, product naming, and metadata—while paying multiple six-figure contracts.

Exit Strategy

CMS Health Check

Use this checklist to evaluate whether your current CMS is ready for the AI-first future—or if it's time to plan your exit strategy.

1. AI-Native and Flexible
  • Can your CMS integrate with any AI/LLM provider, or are you locked into one?
  • Can you swap LLMs as market options evolve (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, Mistral)?
  • Does your CMS treat AI as a native layer (metadata, validation, summarization) or a bolt-on plugin?
2. Cost and Commitment
  • Are you locked into multi-year contracts without performance benchmarks?
  • Can you reduce user licenses or modules without penalty?
  • Is the annual cost duplicated across teams without unified governance?
3. Governance and Ownership
  • Do you have conflicting versions of "truth" for brand, tone, product naming, or metadata?
  • Is there a unified governance structure across teams (legal, marketing, product, support)?
  • Can you maintain consistent taxonomy across all content systems?
4. Interoperability & Extensibility
  • Does the CMS play well with your TMS, DAM, analytics, or design system?
  • Is there robust API support and webhook automation?
  • Can workflows, AI triggers, and validation rules be embedded seamlessly?
5. Migration Readiness
  • Can you export structured content (Markdown, XML, JSON) for reuse elsewhere?
  • Do you have documentation of schemas, workflows, and dependencies?
  • Is there an exit clause that gives you your content and its structure?
